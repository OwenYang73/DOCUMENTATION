Background

Since being formed in 1980, the Phoenix-based Make-A-Wish Foundation has become synonymous with fostering goodwill toward children with serious illnesses. Anyone under the age of 18 can petition the organization with a request for a trip, celebrity visit, or other special arrangement. In almost all cases, they’ll grant the child’s wish via fundraising efforts and the generosity of volunteers. An average of one wish is granted every 17 minutes.

Description of Make-A-Wish

"Make-A-Wish creates life-changing wishes for children with critical illnesses. Our mission is to grant the wish of every eligible child – a quest sparked by the belief that a wish is integral to a child’s treatment."

Key Information and Statistics:

Introduction Description:

Make-A-Wish® – the global organization that creates life-changing wishes for children with critical illnesses – is thrilled to announce a Guinness World Record title achievement of being named the Largest wish-granting non-profit organization. Since the creation of the organization in 1980, Make-A-Wish has granted nearly 450,000 wishes to children with critical illnesses around the world. Incredibly, a wish is granted every 17 minutes globally with the help of more than 40,000 volunteers and countless donors. Last year alone, Make-A-Wish granted more than 30,000 wishes to children in more than 50 countries worldwide.

User Research:

Identification of User Groups:

In order to identify and research the user group of Make-A-Wish, I looked at the social engagements between the charity's Facebook and Instagram accounts. This shows the demographic of volunteers within this charity can be saperate into 3 main groups: teenagers (16-24), adults (25-50), and elders (60-80). Whilst the demographic for donors can be assumed as people with stable income and similarly aged to volunteers. Moreover, their promotional videos on Youtube and their official website also portrays this statement.

Ideation

The ideation stage is the beginning stages of the design process which help enable designers to uncover their final presentation.

  • Mood boards

  • Sitemaps

  • sketches

Mood Boards

Moodboards are imperative within the ideation process as they enable to visualise and draft the direction the appearance and potential styles of the website.

Sitemaps

Sitemaps allows me to understand and plan out the contents that will be used for my website. It also helps me to explore different types of contents that I could consider to add on and find out the most appropriate layout of the website

Sketches

My first iteration consisted of sketches allowed me to quickly brainstorm many ideas for the concept and the layout of the design. These sketches allows me to explore different structures and elements that inspired by the mood board.

Home Landing Page:

Final concept

I decided to go with this design as not only was it simplistic, but it also contain the main information that I would like to deliver to the user. As there will be placing several wishes, descriptions, and pictures on the first landing board, this allows user to easily access to the main goal of Make-A-Wish.

Our impact page:

This page is aimed to let the user understand what Make-A-Wish contributes to the community and the world. This page will demonstrate various a world map of their impact globally and statistics/data inforgraphic styles to demonstrate the impact of Make-A-Wish.

Final Decision

In order to deliver all the information about Make-A-Wish's impact, I decide to combine both 3 and 4 design together. This allows users to see the stories and wishes from different children, access to the statistics of (how many children has been supported by) the organisation, and their location on the world map.

Your impact page:

The purpose of this page is to encourage users to donate, and understand how their donation can be used to help those children who are experiencing life-threatening illness. My design focus was on how to present this information in a simple and clean style, which allows the user to easily understand the statistics of the foundation.

Final Decision

I decide to use design 4 as my final design. As this page's aim is to simply shows how the users' money will support the children.

Donate page:

The purpose of this page is to encourage users to donate. In order to attract user's attention on how their money going to support the kids, I designed that when the user tap on the amount of money they would like to donate, they can see how the money going to make a change to the child. For example, $30 could help create wish anticipation, helping a child have fun rather than focusing on their treatment.(Make-A-Wish, 2020)

Final Decision

I decide to use design 2 as it clean and simple. It also conduct the information of how the user going to help the kids directly. This allows users to easily understand how the donate page work. The right side menu of how their donation can help the children can increase their willing to donate.

Designs

Following the development and exploration of my sketches, I developed my design via both wireframes that presenting the overall structure and basic form of my proposed website mockups to convey the visual style of the website.

Wireframes

Wireframes are sketches that have been drawn over many iterations with a high degree of fidelity. This step is very important in the design process for solidification mockups.

Mockups

Mockups are a high-fidelity display of the website before prototyping. This helps utilise and showcase the style guides with the wireframes created prior. (All images used in the mockup are photos by Make-A-Wish Australia)
